🌱 Learn Islam

Fundamentals of Islam for every new Muslim & seeker

أَهْلاً وَسَهْلاً
Welcome! This section is designed to help new Muslims and those curious about Islam learn the essentials of the faith — step by step, clearly and simply. 🤲
›
The 5 Pillars of Islam — أَرْكَانُ الإِسْلَام
☝️
الشَّهَادَة
1. Shahada — Declaration of Faith
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ
"I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah."
This is the entry point into Islam. Saying this sincerely with full belief makes a person a Muslim. It means complete submission to Allah alone and following the Prophet ﷺ.
🙏
الصَّلَاة
2. Salah — Daily Prayer
Muslims pray 5 times every day — Fajr (dawn), Dhuhr (midday), Asr (afternoon), Maghrib (sunset), Isha (night).
Prayer is a direct connection between the servant and Allah. It is the pillar of the religion — if it is firm, everything is firm. Performed facing the Qiblah (Makkah).
💰
الزَّكَاة
3. Zakat — Obligatory Charity
Giving 2.5% of one's savings annually to those in need — the poor, the needy, and others specified in the Quran.
Zakat purifies one's wealth and soul. It is obligatory on every Muslim who owns wealth above the Nisab (minimum threshold) for one full lunar year.
🌙
الصَّوْم
4. Sawm — Fasting in Ramadan
Fasting the entire month of Ramadan — from Fajr (dawn) to Maghrib (sunset) — abstaining from food, drink and intimate relations.
Fasting trains patience, gratitude, and God-consciousness (Taqwa). It is one of the greatest acts of worship. Allah said: "Fasting is for Me, and I will reward it."
🕋
الحَجّ
5. Hajj — Pilgrimage to Makkah
Performing Hajj to Makkah once in a lifetime, for every Muslim who is physically and financially able.
Hajj is the greatest gathering of Muslims on earth. It symbolises unity, equality before Allah, and remembrance of the Day of Judgement.
The 6 Articles of Faith — أَرْكَانُ الإِيمَان
آمَنتُ بِاللَّهِ وَمَلَائِكَتِهِ وَكُتُبِهِ وَرُسُلِهِ وَالْيَوْمِ الآخِرِ وَبِالْقَدَرِ خَيْرِهِ وَشَرِّهِ
"I believe in Allah, His Angels, His Books, His Messengers, the Last Day, and in Qadar — its good and its evil." — From Hadith Jibril
1️⃣
الإِيمَانُ بِاللَّه
Belief in Allah
Believing Allah is One — with no partner, no son, no equal. He is the Creator, Sustainer and Lord of everything that exists.
2️⃣
الإِيمَانُ بِالمَلَائِكَة
Belief in Angels
Angels are created from light. They do not disobey Allah. Among them: Jibreel (revelation), Mikael (rain), Israfeel (trumpet on Judgement Day).
3️⃣
الإِيمَانُ بِالكُتُب
Belief in Allah's Books
Allah revealed scriptures to His prophets. The Quran is the final, preserved book — the complete and unaltered word of Allah.
4️⃣
الإِيمَانُ بِالرُّسُل
Belief in the Prophets & Messengers
Allah sent prophets to every nation — from Adam to Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, and the final prophet Muhammad ﷺ.
5️⃣
الإِيمَانُ بِاليَوْمِ الآخِر
Belief in the Last Day
A day will come when all creation is resurrected and judged. Every person will stand before Allah and be held accountable for their deeds.
6️⃣
الإِيمَانُ بِالقَدَر
Belief in Qadar (Divine Decree)
Everything that happens — good or bad — occurs by Allah's knowledge and will. A Muslim accepts both ease and hardship with faith and patience.
How to Perform Wudu & Salah
💧 STEP-BY-STEP WUDU (Ablution)
1
Intention (Niyyah) — Make intention in your heart to purify yourself for Salah.
2
Say Bismillah — بِسْمِ اللَّه
3
Wash both hands up to the wrists — 3 times
4
Rinse mouth — 3 times
5
Sniff water into nose and blow out — 3 times
6
Wash face completely — 3 times
7
Wash right arm up to elbow — 3 times, then left arm — 3 times
8
Wipe head with wet hands (front to back once), then wipe ears
9
Wash right foot up to and including the ankle thoroughly, ensuring water reaches between toes — then left foot the same way
✅
Say the Shahada after Wudu: أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ...
🙏 HOW TO PERFORM SALAH
① Stand (Qiyam) — Face the Qiblah
اللَّهُ أَكْبَر
Say Takbeer: "Allahu Akbar" — raise both hands to shoulders
② Recite Al-Fatihah
Recite Surah Al-Fatihah (the opening chapter), then another surah or ayah.
③ Bow (Ruku)
سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْعَظِيم
Say 3x: "Subhana Rabbiyal Adheem" — Glory be to my Lord, the Almighty
④ Prostrate (Sujood)
سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الأَعْلَى
Say 3x: "Subhana Rabbiyal A'la" — Glory be to my Lord, the Most High. Forehead, nose, both hands, knees and toes on ground.
⑤ Tashahhud & Tasleem
Sit and recite At-Tashahhud, then end the prayer by turning right then left saying: السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّه

اللَّهُمَّ بِكَ أَصْبَحْنَا، وَبِكَ أَمْسَيْنَا، وَبِكَ نَحْيَا، وَبِكَ نَمُوتُ وَإِلَيْكَ النُّشُورُ

⑧ — 4 times
اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَصْبَحْتُ أُشْهِدُكَ، وَأُشْهِدُ حَمَلَةَ عَرْشِكَ، وَمَلاَئِكَتِكَ، وَجَمِيعَ خَلْقِكَ، أَنَّكَ أَنْتَ اللَّهُ لَا إِلَهَ إِلاَّ أَنْتَ وَحْدَكَ لاَ شَرِيكَ لَكَ، وَأَنَّ مُحَمَّداً عَبْدُكَ وَرَسُولُكَ

اللَّهُمَّ مَا أَصْبَحَ بِي مِنْ نِعْمَةٍ أَوْ بِأَحَدٍ مِنْ خَلْقِكَ فَمِنْكَ وَحْدَكَ لاَ شَرِيكَ لَكَ، فَلَكَ الْحَمْدُ وَلَكَ الشُّكْرُ

الزَّكَاة
What is Zakat?
Zakat (الزكاة) is the Third Pillar of Islam — an obligatory annual act of worship where Muslims give 2.5% of their qualifying wealth to those in need. The word "Zakat" means purification and growth in Arabic.
📖 Source — Quran & Sunnah
وَأَقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتُوا الزَّكَاةَ وَارْكَعُوا مَعَ الرَّاكِعِينَ
"Establish prayer and give Zakat and bow with those who bow." — Al-Baqarah 2:43
✨ Benefits of Zakat
💚
Purifies wealth — removes the spiritual impurity of hoarding and attachment to dunya
📈
Grows your wealth — Allah promises barakah and increase for those who give Zakat
🤲
Fulfils a pillar — it is a direct act of worship and obedience to Allah
🌍
Helps the Ummah — supports the poor, the needy, travellers and those in debt
🛡️
Protection on Judgement Day — the Prophet ﷺ said Zakat will be a shade for the believer
📋 Am I Obligated to Pay Zakat?
Zakat becomes obligatory when ALL of these conditions are met:
①
You are a Muslim
②
You are of sound mind and adult (reached puberty)
③
Your wealth has reached the Nisab (minimum threshold)
④
One full lunar year (Hawl) has passed on that wealth
